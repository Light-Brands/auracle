# LightField Institute — Brand Identity Guide

## 1. Brand Essence

### Vision

To create a body of published work, healing practice, and education that helps sensitive adults decode patterns of control, heal their nervous systems, reclaim their energy fields, and build lives of sovereign connection.

### Mission

LightField Institute publishes transformational book series, offers one-on-one healing sessions, and delivers structured courses — bridging clinical psychology, somatic healing, and energy work into a single, integrated practice.

### Core Values

- **Sovereignty:** Every person has the right to their own energy, choices, and identity. We teach self-authority, not dependence.
- **Embodiment:** Insight alone doesn't heal. The body must learn something new. We prioritize somatic and nervous system work alongside cognitive understanding.
- **Integrity:** We teach from lived experience and clinical training. No shortcuts, no spiritual bypassing, no false promises.
- **Clarity:** We name things precisely. Patterns, tactics, dynamics — when you can name it, it loses power over you.
- **Luminosity:** The "light field" is both literal (the human biofield) and metaphorical (the clarity that comes from doing the work). We move toward light, not away from darkness.

### Positioning Statement

LightField Institute is the research, publishing, and practice home of Jennifer Brooke Lawless — a therapist, writer, and energy practitioner who bridges clinical psychology and energetic healing. The Institute houses multiple book series, healing sessions, and educational programs, all united by one premise: **you were never broken; you were decoded, and now you are choosing.**

### Book Distribution & Digital Presence

Books are sold through external platforms — Amazon (Kindle and paperback) and Gumroad (PDF/ebook). The website is not a reading platform. Instead, each volume detail page offers:
- Buy links (Amazon, Gumroad)
- A Chapter 1 preview on-site (taste, not the full book)
- A free Chapter 1 download in exchange for email (lead magnet alongside Decoder Cards)

### Testimonials & Social Proof

All testimonials must be real. Currently available: approximately 4 video testimonials and 3 written testimonials. No fabricated or inflated counters. The one real stat — 100+ readings — belongs as a quiet credential in Jennifer's practitioner bio on The Auracle page, not as a flashy homepage banner. Design testimonial placements across the site to accommodate a small but growing collection — featured on the home page, The Auracle page, and volume detail pages where applicable.

### The Auracle — Brand Consolidation

"The Auracle" is a memorable word-of-mouth name combining "oracle" and "aura." It carries real recognition — people already refer others to "The Auracle" by name. Rather than maintaining it as a separate brand with its own visual identity, The Auracle lives as a **dedicated page within LightField Institute** where all session bookings originate.

**What The Auracle IS:**
- A page at `lightfield.institute/the-auracle`
- Jennifer's practitioner title — "Jennifer Brooke Lawless, The Auracle"
- The destination people land on when friends say "go see The Auracle"
- A customer acquisition channel powered by word-of-mouth

**What The Auracle is NOT:**
- A separate brand with its own logo, palette, or visual identity
- A rose-pink, golden-sun, "gentle sacred" aesthetic (retired)
- A standalone website or social media presence
- A competing brand alongside LightField Institute

**The Auracle page uses LightField's visual identity** — kingfisher palette, Cormorant Garamond + Inter typography, cream backgrounds, indigo immersive sections. No separate design system.

**Booking flow:** Connect-first, not transactional. Visitors read about session types, then reach out via WhatsApp or email to connect with Jennifer personally before booking. This filters for readiness, builds relationship before the session, and positions the work as premium and personal — not commodity.

### Why "LightField Institute"

The name bridges every pillar of the platform:

| Pillar | How "Light Field" Connects |
|--------|---------------------------|
| **The Sovereignty Series** | Your field of personal power, agency, and self-authority |
| **The Energy Series** | Your literal biofield — the human energy field you learn to read, clear, and protect |

| **Future work** | The "field" metaphor scales to any domain of consciousness |

"Institute" conveys authority, research-backed credibility, and a container larger than any single book. It positions Jennifer not as an author selling books, but as the founder of a body of work.

## 3. Target Audience

### Primary

Adults (predominantly women, 28–55) healing from narcissistic abuse, codependency, or enmeshed family systems who are:
- Emotionally sensitive and self-aware
- Frustrated that traditional therapy or self-help hasn't fully healed them
- Ready to move from surviving to thriving
- Seeking frameworks that combine psychology with somatic and energetic approaches

### Secondary

- Therapists, coaches, and healers seeking frameworks for their own clients
- Partners and family members of survivors seeking understanding
- Adults exploring energy work, aura reading, and spiritual development with clinical grounding

### Psychographic Profile

They've done some work. They can name what happened to them. But their nervous system still reacts. They want to understand *why* awareness alone isn't enough, and they want practical tools — not platitudes — to build secure connection from the inside out.

## 4. Visual Identity

### 4A. Logo & Wordmark

**Primary mark:** "LightField Institute" as a clean wordmark. The existing sparkle/star logo element from the current site should be refined and rendered in electric blue or indigo.

**Logo rules:**
- The wordmark uses the heading typeface (Cormorant Garamond) for "LightField" and the body typeface (Inter) for "Institute"
- "LightField" is one word, no space, capital L and F
- Minimum clear space: the height of the "L" on all sides
- The sparkle element can be used as a standalone icon (favicon, app icon, social avatar)

### 4B. Color Palette — Inspired by the Kingfisher

The entire visual system is drawn from the common kingfisher (*Alcedo atthis*) — a bird whose plumage is electric blue along the back, deep indigo on the wings, fiery orange-red on the breast, and soft cream at the throat. The colors are not produced by pigment but by structural refraction of light — fitting for a brand called LightField.

#### Primary Colors

| Color | Hex | Role |
|-------|-----|------|
| **Cream** | `#F5F0E1` | Primary background — warmth, openness, breathing room |
| **Black** | `#1A1A1A` | Primary text, headlines — sharpness, authority |
| **Dark Indigo** | `#0C1A2E` | Immersive sections (hero, footer) — depth, gravitas |
| **Indigo** | `#13417C` | Secondary dark — cards on dark backgrounds, overlays |

#### Accent Colors

| Color | Hex | Role |
|-------|-----|------|
| **Electric Blue** | `#0EA5C3` | Primary accent — links, interactive elements, the "flash" |
| **Orange-Red** | `#E0522D` | CTA accent — buttons, urgency, action, fire |
| **Warm Orange** | `#FB9543` | Highlight accent — warmth, midtone between red and cream |

#### Series-Specific Accents

| Series | Color | Hex | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----|-----------|
| **The Sovereignty Series** | Orange-Red | `#E0522D` | Fire, truth, the courage to see and stand |
| **The Energy Series** | Electric Blue | `#0EA5C3` | The biofield, light, energetic clarity |
| **Future Series** | TBD | TBD | Drawn from remaining palette tones |

#### Semantic Colors

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Success | `#3A8A5C` | Available, confirmed |
| Warning | `#FB9543` | Coming soon, limited |
| Info | `#0EA5C3` | Informational, tips |
| Error | `#C4421F` | Error states |

#### Usage Rhythm

The palette creates a natural breathing rhythm across all touchpoints:

**Cream (breathe) → Indigo (immerse) → Cream (breathe)**

Like the kingfisher diving into water and emerging again. Cream is the default. Dark indigo is reserved for impact moments — hero sections, footers, series banners, newsletter strips. The site never defaults to an all-dark theme.

#### The Kingfisher Rule

The kingfisher bird itself should **never** appear literally in brand materials. The palette IS the bird. If a subtle nod is desired, a single abstract feather illustration or light-refraction pattern can echo the source — but the bird stays implied, never illustrated.

### 4C. Typography

| Role | Font | Weight | Fallback |
|------|------|--------|----------|
| **Headings** | Cormorant Garamond | 400, 500 | Georgia, serif |
| **Body** | Inter | 300, 400, 500 | system-ui, sans-serif |
| **Arc Words** | Cormorant Garamond (uppercase, tracked) | 500, 600 | Georgia, serif |
| **Monospace** | JetBrains Mono | 400 | monospace |

**Why this pairing:** Cormorant Garamond brings editorial elegance and spiritual warmth to headings. Inter provides clean, modern readability for body text. The contrast between serif headings and sans-serif body creates a magazine-like editorial feel that positions the brand above typical self-help aesthetics.

**Arc Word treatment:** The single-word titles (SEE, HEAL, STAND, etc.) are the brand's most recognizable typographic element. They are always set in Cormorant Garamond, uppercase, with 0.25em letter-spacing. They should dominate any layout they appear in.

### 4D. Imagery & Art Direction

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Hero backgrounds** | Deep indigo with abstract light effects — light refracting through water. Electric blue and orange-red light streaks on dark indigo. No stock photos of people. |
| **Book covers** | High-quality 3D mockups with shadows on cream backgrounds. Slight angle, not flat. |
| **Session imagery** | Abstract or environmental — water, light through prisms, nature. Never photos of people in sessions. Water imagery is natural (kingfisher is a river bird). |
| **Author photo** | AI-enhanced professional headshot from real photo. Warm, approachable, not overly staged. Same photo across all materials for consistency. |

## 5. Tone of Voice

### Core Qualities

- **Direct:** We name things. No hedging, no euphemism. "This was abuse" not "this was a difficult dynamic."
- **Grounded:** Clinical precision without clinical coldness. We cite the body, the nervous system, the research — and we also honor the lived experience.
- **Warm:** Authority with compassion. We write as someone who has been there, not as someone observing from outside.
- **Empowering:** Every piece of content moves the reader toward self-authority. We never create dependence on the brand or the author.

### Voice Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Use imperative verbs: "Name it. See it. Heal it."
- Speak to the reader's intelligence — they don't need to be convinced they were hurt
- Ground abstract concepts in the body: "your nervous system," "your breath," "your field"
- Use the arc words as shorthand: "This is a SEE moment" or "You're in STAND territory now"

**Don't:**
- Use clinical jargon without explanation
- Pathologize the reader — they are not broken, they were conditioned
- Promise quick fixes — this work takes time and practice
- Use "woo" language without substance — no ungrounded mysticism
- Overuse emojis, exclamation marks, or forced enthusiasm

### Example Phrases

- "Every pattern you can name is a pattern that loses power over you."
- "Insight alone doesn't heal. The body needs to learn something new."
- "You were never broken. You were decoded."
- "Your field speaks. I help you listen."
- "Where seen and unseen meet."

If LightField Institute were a person, they would be:

- A therapist who has done their own deep work and speaks from experience, not theory
- Someone who can hold space without absorbing your pain
- Precise with language — names things exactly, never minimizes
- Warm but not soft — compassionate authority, not gentle passivity
- Someone who believes in your sovereignty before you do

### We are NOT:

- A clinical therapy website (no stock photos of couches or handshakes)
- A "woo" crystal shop (no rainbows, no clip art, no Comic Sans)
- A basic WordPress blog with a book sidebar
- An Amazon listing page dressed up as a website
- A guru cult — we build self-authority, not followers

### We ARE:

- An institute — a body of research, practice, and published work
- A sanctuary — a warm, luminous space to explore difficult truths
- A bookshelf — a growing library organized by series and journey
- A practitioner's home — where you come to work with Jennifer directly, through The Auracle
- A kingfisher — precise, vivid, alive. Dives deep, emerges with clarity

## 9. Usage Guidelines

### The Name

- **Full:** LightField Institute (preferred for formal contexts)
- **Short:** LightField (acceptable in casual contexts after first full mention)
- **Never:** Light Field (two words), Lightfield (no capital F), LFI (no abbreviation)

### Color Integrity

- Cream (`#F5F0E1`) is the default background for all materials. Never use pure white (`#FFFFFF`) as a primary background.
- Dark indigo (`#0C1A2E`) is for immersive moments only — never as the default.
- Orange-red (`#E0522D`) is reserved for calls to action and the Sovereignty Series. Do not use it decoratively.
- Electric blue (`#0EA5C3`) is for interactive elements and the Energy Series. It should always feel like a flash of color, not a flood.

### Typography Integrity

- Arc words (SEE, HEAL, STAND, etc.) are always uppercase, always in Cormorant Garamond, always with letter-spacing.
- Never set arc words in the body font (Inter).
- Never set arc words in lowercase.
- The arc words are the brand's most distinctive visual element — protect them.

### Photography

- Never use stock photos with people (looks cheap at this genre level)
- Never use borders or frames on images (can look dated)
- Author headshot is the only photo of a person used across the brand
- Abstract, natural, and light-based imagery only
