# Sensitivity-Reader Intent (Vol 2: HEAL)

## Purpose

This document states the author's **intent** for cultural-coverage work in Vol 2. It is a working guideline — not a process gate. The author will seek reader input from the six communities named below and will do the community-voice work with care. This document records how that work is meant to happen so the intent is legible to collaborators, editors, and future readers of the repo. It does not block commits, PRs, or drafting.

The intent exists because the author does not hold first-person authority in the six communities this plan covers. Drafting community-internal voice without reader input from the community is where books like this one most often fail their readers, and the author is writing with that awareness.

## Scope

This intent applies to the six communities named in `cultural-coverage-integration-plan.md`:

1. Black / African American
2. Asian (East, South, Southeast)
3. Indigenous / Native
4. Middle Eastern / Arab / SWANA
5. Religious traditions (Evangelical, Mormon/LDS, JW, Catholic — depth)
6. Transgender-specific dynamics

It also applies, by extension, to any future community added to the coverage plan where the author does not hold direct first-person authority.

It does **not** apply to:

- Jewish (Ashkenazi) or Jewrican-by-marriage material — the author holds first-person authority here.
- Hispanic / Latino, LGBTQ+ (not trans-specific), immigrant, and male-survivor material already integrated in Vol 1 per `../vol-1-see/unified-woven-integration-plan.md`. That pass already went through its reader process.

## The Intent

For the six in-scope communities, the author's working intent is to have reader input from that community when writing:

- First-person field notes in a community member's voice (composite or verified)
- Scripts or dialogue containing community-internal phrasing, in-language idioms, or code-switching
- Direct claims about community-internal theology, ceremony, kinship rules, gender codes, or honor architecture
- Community-specific exit pathways (e.g., JW judicial-committee procedure, LDS temple-recommend mechanics, Catholic annulment specifics, tribal disenrollment)
- In-language terms used with emotional weight (not just glossed in passing)

The following content is squarely author-voice / structural / factual, and the author will continue drafting it as usual:

- Structural patterns named from the outside, without putting words in any community's mouth (the Ch 5, 6, 7, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16, 18, 19, 22 landed passages are the template)
- Verified directory resources (organization names, hotlines, URLs) on the companion website
- Author-voice pointer language directing readers to community-specific companion resources
- Cross-community framing that names the shape of a pattern without prescribing how it reads from inside

## The Working Process

1. **Reader recruitment** is tracked in the table in `cultural-coverage-integration-plan.md`. The author is actively seeking readers for each of the six communities.
2. **Outline with the reader where possible.** When a reader is in the loop for a community, the author shares the outline for that community's material before drafting prose.
3. **Drafting, one community at a time**, when community-voice material is on the table. The author will not parallel-draft community-voice sections for multiple communities, because community-internal material for one community can read against another's, and a single-focus pass makes the cross-read tractable.
4. **Sage (AI) as a second pass.** The `book/agents/sensitivity-reader.md` agent is run over Vol 2 community-coverage material as a supplemental read. Sage does not replace a human reader from the community; it catches what the author's own revision passes might have missed and flags items for the human reader's attention.
5. **Integration pass.** Community-voice material lands in the Vol 2 chapters mapped in the integration plan.
6. **Cross-community review.** Where readers are in the loop, each reviews the integrated manuscript — not just their own contribution — to catch where material reads against another community's material.
7. **Legal review** paralleling the Tier 1/2/3 structure used for author-identifying details in Vol 1.

## Examples

Things the author holds as community-voice work, best done with reader input:

- A first-person paragraph in the voice of a Black survivor.
- A script in which a Korean mother speaks to her daughter about shame.
- A passage describing the internal mechanics of a JW judicial committee with confidence.
- A field note in the voice of a trans woman describing HRT-continuity-as-leverage inside a coercive relationship.
- Using "aguanta," "sabr," "chī kǔ," "shalhevet," or similar in-language terms with emotional weight in a passage that is *about* that community.

Things that are author-voice / structural / factual, no reader required:

- An author-voice paragraph naming that in many Asian, SWANA, and Latin family systems, emotional restraint was taught as maturity — without writing a scene inside any of those family systems.
- Listing verified organization names and URLs on the companion website for Indigenous, SWANA, JW-exit, and trans-specific resources, without drafting survivor-voice testimony.
- A structural description (as in Ch 12's "When the Community Is the Relationship") of the shape of the cost, without composing community-internal voice.
- Quoting language the author's own communities use — Jewish, Jewrican-by-marriage — because the author holds authority there.

## Exceptions and Edge Cases

- **Published, attributable source material** (a quote from a memoir, an interview, a clinical paper) used with full citation and in the source's own voice is not "putting words in a community's mouth"; it is quoting a community member speaking for themselves. Fair-use and permissions rules still apply.
- **The author's own first-person material** in the communities the author holds authority in is not in scope of this intent in the first place.
- **Direct reader-contributed material** sent to the author for inclusion, used with documented consent, appears in the reader's own voice.

## A Note on Why This Is an Intent, Not a Gate

This document used to read as a hard policy gate. It has been relaxed to an author's intent so work on Vol 2 can move without process friction while the reader-recruitment work continues in parallel. The care the document describes still matters — community-voice harm doesn't surface as a line-edit issue; it surfaces as a reader from that community closing the book — and the author holds that care as the reason to do this work thoughtfully. It simply does not need to be enforced as a commit gate to be real.
