# Cultural Coverage Integration Plan — Vol 2: HEAL

## Status (2026-04-16)

This document is the **Vol-2-specific blueprint** for integrating the six communities named in `../vol-1-see/cultural-coverage-gaps-proposal.md`:

1. **Black / African American**
2. **Asian (East, South, Southeast)**
3. **Indigenous / Native**
4. **Middle Eastern / Arab**
5. **Religious traditions** (Evangelical, Mormon/LDS, JW, Catholic — depth)
6. **Transgender-specific dynamics**

The Vol 1 proposal mapped these communities onto Vol 1's recognition-focused chapters. This document re-maps them onto Vol 2's recovery-focused architecture, because **recognition is relatively universal across cultures; recovery is where cultural specificity actually changes the prescription**. This is why the hybrid decision documented at the top of `../vol-1-see/cultural-coverage-gaps-proposal.md` deferred the depth treatment to Vol 2.

## What Is Explicitly NOT Drafted Here (and Why)

To be transparent about scope, this pass deliberately does not draft:

- **First-person community-voice field notes.** The author has direct authority on Jewish (Ashkenazi) and Jewrican-by-marriage experience. She does not have direct authority on Black, Asian, Indigenous, Middle Eastern, JW/LDS/Evangelical/Catholic, or trans experience. Drafting first-person field notes in those voices without sensitivity readers in the loop is exactly what the proposal warns against on line 816.
- **Community-specific scripts** containing in-language phrasing, idioms, or culturally-specific dialogue. Same reason.
- **Per-tradition theological detail** beyond the structural pattern (e.g., specific Mormon temple-recommend mechanics, JW judicial-committee procedures, Catholic annulment specifics). Each requires reader review by someone inside that tradition.

These items are **not abandoned** — they are **scheduled** behind the sensitivity-reader gate. This document plus `../vol-1-see/cultural-coverage-gaps-proposal.md` together constitute the brief each sensitivity reader will work from.

## Implementation Phases (After This Pass)

1. **Sensitivity reader recruitment** — six readers, per the table above. Recommended order: start with communities where the author has the least direct knowledge (Indigenous, JW, trans), so their input shapes the framework before drafting deepens.
2. **Per-community drafting** — one community at a time, with that community's reader in the loop from outline → draft → revision.
3. **Integration pass** — merge each community's drafted material into the Vol 2 chapters mapped above.
4. **Sage review** (existing pipeline).
5. **Final reader pass** — each community's reader reviews the integrated manuscript, not just their own contribution, to catch where their material reads against another community's material.
6. **Legal review** on integrated material, paralleling the Tier 1/2/3 structure used for author-identifying details in Vol 1.

## A Note on Voice

The unified plan's "Voice Strategy: First-Person vs. Anonymous" section (`../vol-1-see/unified-woven-integration-plan.md` § Voice Strategy) applies to Vol 2 as well. The author's first-person Jewrican spine extends into Vol 2's recovery chapters (especially Ch 12, 13, 15, 22). All other community material in Vol 2 is anonymous composite, and each composite must be sourced through that community's sensitivity reader rather than imagined.

The trust chain is the same: the author's own first-person material establishes "this person knows," and the composites then ride that trust — but only if the composites are real, not invented.
