# Round 6 — Sage Sensitivity Refresh (Vol 1)

A focused, single-reviewer sensitivity pass run after the Round 5 Sage
implementation was closed. This is not a full review round; it's a targeted
second-pass filter per the standing rule in `book/agents/sensitivity-reader.md`
— the author's decisions have been implemented, and Sage is reading the
resulting prose to catch what a first draft of the changes may have missed.

## Contents

- `sage-sensitivity-reader-issue.md` — the review itself, 12 questions focused
  on what the current manuscript surfaces now that Round 5 decisions have
  landed.
- `author-decisions-sage-sensitivity.md` — template for author responses;
  one selection per question plus optional reasoning.

## How this differs from Round 5

Round 5 was a broad sensitivity scan before post-publication refinement. This
refresh runs against the updated manuscript and focuses on:

1. Prose fit and tone of the newly added representation passages — are they
   integrated or do they read as bolted-on disclaimers?
2. Items flagged in `review-05/phase-2-content/sage-implementation-log.md`
   as partial (Q4, Q11 mid-lesson, Q13 normalization framing, Q18 survivor-
   centering).
3. New concerns surfaced by a read of the current manuscript that Round 5
   did not identify, including the author's own voice and case-study
   pattern.

The goal is catch rather than rework.
