## Diana (Developmental Editor) — 15 Questions

### Section A: Architecture & Sequencing

**Q1. Part-Level Load Imbalance**
- **Decision:** **C — Split Part II *and* absorb Part IV into Part III.**
- **Rationale:** Two structural anomalies (Part II = 7 chapters, Part IV = 1 chapter) resolve in one move. Resulting six-part shape: I Recognition / II Origins / III Family Systems / IV Patterns in Context / V Tools / VI Recovery & Response. Each Part lands at 2–4 chapters. The asymmetry isn't carrying meaning; it's residue.
- **Author Note:** Diana drafts the split point and Part-name candidates. Pause-cadence question (Q2) re-evaluates after this.

**Q2. Missing Pause Before Part V**
- **Decision:** **C — Move the post-Ch-14 Pause to before Ch 15.**
- **Rationale:** Four Pauses is the right cadence. The current placement clusters two Pauses around Ch 14 and leaves the biggest tonal hinge (cognition → embodiment) without one. Moving the existing Pause is cheaper than drafting a new one and preserves the rhythm.
- **Author Note:** Re-evaluate after Q1 restructure lands — Part renumbering may shift Pause anchors.

**Q3. Chapter 14's Dual Identity**
- **Decision:** **A — Ch 14 = Emergency Protocol only; update the ToC entry.**
- **Rationale:** The cards live in Ch 3 (where the reader meets them in narrative context). Ch 14 is the in-the-moment use protocol. The ToC's card sub-bullets are drift from an earlier draft and should be cut. Lightest fix that resolves the navigation failure.

**Q4. Closing Beat of Chapter 17**
- **Decision:** **C — Both: move the bio paragraph to About the Author *and* draft a new closing line.**
- **Rationale:** The "superpower" line is the third occurrence; repetition has dulled it. The bio paragraph mixes registers (emotional close + biographical update). Both fixes are independent and both improve the ending.
- **Author Note:** Diana drafts three candidate closing lines pulling from different threads the book has earned.

### Section B: Thesis & Through-Line

**Q5. Woven Thesis Surfaces Once**
- **Decision:** **C — Both: promote into Ch 1 *and* distribute into Part headers.**
- **Rationale:** The integration plan calls the woven thesis "the spine." A spine appearing once in front matter isn't a spine. Ch 1 names the thesis explicitly; Part headers reinforce it at four hinge points. Together they make the thesis load-bearing.
- **Author Note:** Risk that the thesis starts to feel "argued" rather than "walked through" is mitigated by keeping the language voice-driven, not academic. Diana drafts both with that constraint.

**Q6. Cultural Integration Architecture vs. Ornament**
- **Decision:** **E — Distribute more evenly without enforcing a structural template.**
- **Rationale:** Forced cadence ("every chapter must mention three cultures") becomes performative quickly and a sensitive reader smells it. But Ch 4 (Trauma Bonds), Ch 5 (Narcissist Types), and Ch 9 (Childhood Patterns) at zero instances each is too thin given the integration plan's premise. Add 6–8 field notes targeted to the current-zero chapters. Don't impose a recurring chapter element.

**Q7. Recovery Arc's Cultural Silence**
- **Decision:** **C — Both: distributed field notes in Ch 15/16 *and* a "Recovery Across Contexts" section in Ch 17.**
- **Rationale:** The integration plan's signature critique is that existing narcissism literature assumes individualist recovery. If Vol 1 replicates that assumption, the book's signature contribution is unfulfilled. The reader who's been validated through Ch 7's collectivist diagnosis must still be seen at Ch 17's recovery. This is the single highest-leverage developmental change in Round 7.
- **Author Note:** Pairs with Q5 — both make the woven thesis architectural rather than ornamental.

### Section C: Reader Experience & Pacing

**Q8. Field Note Distribution**
- **Decision:** **C — Both: add to Ch 11 *and* audit/cut Ch 2's 17 instances.**
- **Rationale:** Ch 2 with 17 field notes risks fatigue; Ch 11 (Romantic Manipulation, the highest-traffic chapter for first-time readers) at zero is structurally indefensible. Some Ch 2 notes likely cluster on the same somatic concept and consolidate cleanly; at least three of those notes can support new field notes in Ch 11 with cultural-context anchors.
- **Author Note:** Targets ~12–14 notes in Ch 2, ~5–6 in Ch 11 post-balance. Pairs with Q11.

**Q9. Front Matter Doing the Work of Five Documents**
- **Decision:** **B — Moderate cut: consolidate the six "A Note on…" sections into one "Notes on This Book."**
- **Rationale:** Aggressive cut (A) risks losing duty-of-care content. Current 17-section state is too long and delays the book's opening. B threads the needle: consolidate the Notes (Memory, Language, Diversity & Access, Diagnosis, Access & Privilege, Field Experience) into a single Notes section with subsections. Crisis Triage stays where it is. The Promise stays where it is.
- **Author Note:** Confirm with publisher whether any specific Note language is contractually required (e.g., disclaimer wording) before consolidating.

**Q10. Chapter 3 Carries Too Much Weight**
- **Decision:** **A — Split into Ch 3a (Core Patterns 0–27) and Ch 3b (Advanced & Specialized Patterns 28–51 + D + P + S).**
- **Rationale:** The chapter's existing internal "Part 1 / Part 2 / Part 3" sub-banners already mark the natural split point. A 2,839-line chapter asks too much of a linear reader; splitting honors the dual-purpose function (teaching + reference) by giving each its own chapter. Doesn't disturb Part II.
- **Author Note:** Renumbering propagates downstream — Cora flags every chapter cross-reference for update in Phase 3.

**Q11. Ch 11 Specifically**
- **Decision:** **A — Add 4–6 field notes to Ch 11 spanning integration plan contexts.**
- **Rationale:** Ch 11 is the chapter readers came for; zero field notes is a missed-anchor disaster. Recent Phase 4 voice activations (per commit history) sharpened the chapter's arc but did not substitute for grounded examples. Span: at minimum one each from Hispanic, Jewish, LGBTQ+, religious, immigrant, male-survivor contexts; balance against integration plan's expressed weighting.
- **Author Note:** Some notes can be drawn from Ch 2's audit (Q8). Pairs structurally with Q8.

### Section C: Conflict Pre-Resolution and Decision Authority

**Q7. Diana × Sage Conflict**
- **Decision:** **A — Diana wins on architecture; Sage wins on representation.**
- **Rationale:** Clean precedence rule. Disagreements about whether a passage exists → Diana. Disagreements about what the passage says or how it represents identity → Sage. **Q8. Standing Decision Override**
- **Decision:** **C — Re-ratify standing decisions before reviewers begin.**
- **Rationale:** Cheap re-ratification (one author pass through the integration plan's standing decisions) surfaces any quietly-revised assumptions and gives reviewers a current spec. Without this, every reviewer makes their own assumption about whether a standing decision is binding, and the manuscript inherits inconsistent treatment.
- **Author Note:** Re-ratification is a lightweight document — author marks each of the integration plan's standing decisions as STILL HOLDS / REVISED / RETIRED. Morgan briefs Phase 2/3/4 reviewers against the ratified set.

**Q9. Decision Authority Matrix**
- **Decision:** **A — Conventional split.** (CONFIRMED 2026-04-27)
- **Rationale:** Mechanical → reviewer-autonomous (typo, comma, dictionary spelling). Substantive → reviewer-recommends, author-decides (structure, voice, cut/keep, content additions). Identity/values → author-only with veto power. Standard editorial convention; well-understood by reviewers; balances author bandwidth against reviewer effectiveness.
- **Author Note:** Confirmed 2026-04-27 — author bandwidth is not a constraint for Round 7. Conventional split stands; ~80–120 author decisions across all four phases is acceptable.
