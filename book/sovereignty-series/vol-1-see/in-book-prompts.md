# Volume 1: SEE — In-Book Reflection Prompts

> Light prompts woven into the end of each chapter. These are invitations, not exercises—
> the book still talking to the reader, not assigning homework.
>
> **Tone:** Conversational, grounded, never clinical. 1–3 per chapter.
>
> **Design note:** Set these apart visually with whitespace and italic type.
> No numbered lists, no fill-in-the-blank. Just a question and room to breathe.

---

## Chapter 1 — The Opening Manifesto

*Think about the relationships in your life right now. In which ones do you feel larger afterward—and in which ones do you feel smaller?*

*What is one thing you've been explaining away that your body has been trying to tell you?*

---

## Chapter 2 — The Energetic Signature

*Right now, as you're reading this—where is the tension in your body? Don't try to fix it. Just notice it.*

*Think of a recent conversation that left you drained. What did your body do during it that your mind ignored?*

*After your next interaction with someone important to you, ask yourself one question: Do I feel more like myself, or less?*

---

## Chapter 3 — The Decoder Playbook

*As you read through the patterns, which one made your breath catch? That's your body recognizing something your mind hasn't named yet.*

*What is one pattern you've experienced that you didn't have a word for until now?*

---

## Chapter 4 — The Psychology of Trauma Bonds

*Have you ever confused relief with love? Think about the moment after a fight—when things suddenly felt calm again. Was that peace, or was that the cycle resetting?*

*What would change if you stopped asking "Why can't I leave?" and started asking "What was my nervous system trained to need?"*

---

## Chapter 5 — Understanding Narcissist Types

*When you think of the person who brought you to this book—what was the gap between who they presented themselves to be and who they actually were?*

*What is the smallest criticism that triggered the biggest reaction in them?*

---

## Chapter 6 — The Masks They Wear

*Which role did you find hardest to read—the one that made you think, "But that's just who they are"? That's the mask that fit so well you forgot it was a mask.*

*What would it mean if the role they play has nothing to do with who they are and everything to do with what they need from you?*

---

## Chapter 7 — Family Roles & Triangulation

*Which family role did you just recognize yourself in? Sit with that for a moment before reading further. There's no rush.*

*Who in your family were you never allowed to be close to—and who decided that?*

---

## Chapter 8 — Parental Wounds

*What is the sentence your parent never said that you spent your childhood trying to earn?*

*If your body could speak about your childhood in one sentence, what would it say?*

---

## Chapter 9 — Understanding Your Childhood Patterns

*What did love require from you as a child? Did it require you to perform, to shrink, to manage someone else's feelings, or to disappear?*

*What is one thing you were told was "too sensitive" that was actually accurate perception?*

---

## Chapter 10 — Breaking Free from Family Systems

*Is there a sibling you've been trying to wake up? What would it cost you to stop trying—and what would it give you back?*

*Think about your last family gathering. Were you being yourself, or were you playing the role the system assigned you?*

---

## Chapter 11 — Romantic Manipulation

*Looking back at the beginning of your relationship, what felt like destiny that might have been data-gathering?*

*When did you first start walking on eggshells—and what did you tell yourself it was?*

---

## Chapter 12 — Manipulation Across Contexts

*Outside of your primary relationship, where else have you felt that familiar shrinking? A friendship? A workplace? A spiritual community?*

*What is the relationship no one would suspect—the one everyone thinks is fine—that costs you the most?*

---

## Chapter 13 — The Exposure Questions

*Before you use any of these questions: Are you safe? Check your body, not just your circumstances. Safety isn't just physical—it's whether your nervous system has room to handle what comes next.*

*Which exposure question are you most afraid to ask? That fear is information.*

---

## Chapter 14 — Decoder Cards — Emergency Protocol

*You don't need to memorize anything. You just need to remember one question when you're spinning: "Whose needs are being centered right now?"*

*The next time you feel confused after an interaction, don't try to figure out what happened. Start with what your body is doing.*

---

## Chapter 15 — Energetic Remapping

*What is one thing that is true about you regardless of anyone else's evaluation?*

*Where in your body do you still carry a shame that was never yours to begin with?*

*Recovery is not a destination. What is one small thing you did this week that your nervous system five years ago wouldn't have allowed?*

---

## Chapter 16 — Practical Responses & Scripts

*Think of the conversation you're dreading. What would it feel like to walk in knowing you don't have to win it—you just have to survive it with yourself intact?*

*What is one sentence you wish you could say—not to change them, but to hear yourself say it out loud?*

---

## Chapter 17 — Moving Forward

*What are you grieving that you never actually had?*

*If you put your hand on your heart right now and said, "I believe myself"—what happens in your body?*

*You don't have to be finished healing to close this book. You just have to trust that you've already started.*
