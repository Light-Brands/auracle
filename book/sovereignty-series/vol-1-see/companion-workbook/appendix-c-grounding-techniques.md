# Appendix C: Grounding Techniques Menu

> All techniques from the book in one printable reference.
> Not every technique works for every body. Try them when you're calm first.
> Then you'll know which ones to reach for when you're activated.

---

## When to Use Grounding

Use these techniques when you:
- Feel flooded, panicked, or dissociated
- Can't think clearly after an interaction
- Notice your body is activated (racing heart, shallow breathing, tension)
- Are stuck in a thought loop replaying a conversation
- Need to come back to the present moment before making a decision

**The goal isn't to "feel better."** The goal is to come back to *here* — to your body, to this room, to this moment. The feelings can be addressed once you're grounded.

## Quick Grounding (30 seconds or less)

### The 3-3-3 Rule

- **3 things** you can see — name them out loud
- **3 things** you can hear — name them out loud
- **3 things** you can touch — reach out and feel them

*This works because it anchors your attention in present-moment sensory reality, pulling you out of the loop.*

### The 3-3-3 Breathing Rule (from the book)

- **3 Seconds:** Pause before responding. Count silently.
- **3 Breaths:** Inhale for 4 counts, exhale for 6 counts. Repeat three times.
- **3 Words (or fewer):** "I'll think about that." "Interesting perspective." "Not for me."

*Use this in real-time during interactions with manipulative people.*

### Feet on Floor

- Press your feet into the ground
- Feel the solid surface beneath you
- Notice the weight of your body in the chair or standing
- You are here. You are in this room. You are safe.

### The Squeeze-Release

- Clench all muscles tight for 5 seconds
- Release completely
- Notice the difference between tension and release
- Repeat three times

*Your body can't tell the difference between emotional tension and physical tension. Releasing the physical releases some of the emotional.*

## Breathing Techniques (1-3 minutes)

### Long Exhale Breathing

- Inhale for 4 counts
- Exhale for 6-8 counts
- Repeat 5-10 times

*Extended exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system — your "rest and digest" mode. This is the fastest way to manually calm your nervous system.*

### 4-7-8 Breathing

- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 7 counts
- Exhale for 8 counts
- Repeat 3-4 times

*Particularly effective for anxiety and panic. The long hold and exhale interrupt the fight-or-flight cascade.*

### Box Breathing

- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Repeat 4 times

*Used by first responders and military for acute stress. Simple to remember under pressure.*

## Body-Based Grounding (3-10 minutes)

### The 5-4-3-2-1 Technique

- **5** things you can SEE
- **4** things you can TOUCH (reach out and feel them)
- **3** things you can HEAR
- **2** things you can SMELL
- **1** thing you can TASTE

*This engages all five senses, which pulls your brain into present-moment processing and out of threat-mode.*

### The Butterfly Hug

- Cross arms over chest
- Place hands on opposite shoulders
- Tap alternating sides slowly (left, right, left, right)
- Continue for 1-2 minutes

*Bilateral stimulation calms the nervous system. This is based on EMDR principles and can be done anywhere, discreetly.*

### The Cold Reset

- Hold ice cubes in your hands
- Splash cold water on your face and wrists
- Place a cold cloth on the back of your neck

*Cold sensation activates the dive reflex, which slows heart rate and interrupts emotional flooding. Surprisingly effective for intense activation.*

### The Vocal Release

- Sigh loudly — let the sound come from deep in your body
- Hum — any pitch, sustain it
- Make a sustained "voo" sound (as in "voodoo")
- Sing — even one note

*Vocalization engages the vagus nerve, which runs from your brain through your throat to your organs. Activating it calms the whole system.*

### Physical Discharge

- Shake your hands vigorously for 30 seconds
- Bounce on your feet or do jumping jacks
- Push against a wall with full strength for 10 seconds, then release
- Walk briskly — let your arms swing naturally

*Your body stores activation as energy. Movement helps discharge it. Animals do this instinctively after a threat passes — they shake, then move on.*

## Earth-Based Grounding (10+ minutes)

### Earth Contact

- Go barefoot on grass, soil, or sand
- Stand still and feel the ground receiving your weight
- Let the earth hold you — you don't have to hold yourself
- Stay for at least 10 minutes if possible

### Laying Flat

- Lie on the ground — floor, grass, earth
- Let your full weight release into the surface
- Feel gravity doing its work
- The body remembers: ground is safety

### Water

- Stand or sit in a shower and feel the water on your skin
- Hold your hands under running water — warm or cold
- If near natural water, listen to it, touch it, let the sound regulate you

## Cognitive Grounding (for thought loops)

### Name the Pattern

When your mind is replaying a conversation or moment:

Instead of reliving the words, translate to pattern language:
- "That was gaslighting" (not: replaying exactly what they said)
- "That was shame dumping" (not: analyzing whether they were right)
- "That was DARVO" (not: rehearsing what you should have said)

*Naming interrupts the loop and gives your brain something useful to do.*

### The Reality Anchor

Write down or say aloud:
- **My name is** _______________
- **I am in** _______________ (location)
- **Today is** _______________ (date)
- **I am safe because** _______________
- **One thing I know for certain is** _______________

### The Three Truths

Say aloud three things that are true about you regardless of anyone's evaluation:

1. _______________
2. _______________
3. _______________

*The narcissist destabilized your sense of self by making your worth contingent on their assessment. These truths exist independently of their opinion.*

## For Dissociation Specifically

If you feel disconnected from your body, numb, or "watching yourself from above":

1. **Temperature change:** Hold ice, splash cold water, step outside
2. **Strong sensation:** Bite into a lemon, snap a rubber band on your wrist, stomp your feet
3. **Orientation:** Look around the room. Name what you see. Touch surfaces.
4. **Movement:** Clap your hands. Stamp your feet. Cross your arms and squeeze yourself.
5. **Voice:** Say your name out loud. Say where you are. Hear your own voice.

*Dissociation is your body's way of leaving a situation it can't physically leave. It was protective then. You can come back now.*
