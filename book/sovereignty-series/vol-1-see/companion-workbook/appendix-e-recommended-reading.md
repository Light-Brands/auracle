## Foundational Reading

These books informed the perspective of *See* and are recommended as companions to your journey:

### On Narcissistic Abuse & Manipulation

- **Why Does He Do That?** — Lundy Bancroft
  *The foundational text on abusive behavior. Direct, unflinching, practical. If you read one book besides this one, read this.*

- **The Verbally Abusive Relationship** — Patricia Evans
  *Names the patterns of verbal abuse with precision. Particularly helpful for recognizing what's happening when nothing "physical" has occurred.*

- **Psychopath Free** — Jackson MacKenzie
  *Written by a survivor. Covers love bombing, devaluation, and discard with the specificity of someone who's lived it.*

- **Whole Again** — Jackson MacKenzie
  *The follow-up to Psychopath Free, focused on recovery. Especially useful for Parts IV and V of this workbook.*

- **Should I Stay or Should I Go?** — Ramani Durvasula
  *Practical guidance for the decision that dominates the middle of recovery.*

- **Will I Ever Be Good Enough?** — Karyl McBride
  *Specifically addresses the mother wound. If Chapter 8 activated you, this book goes deeper.*

### On Trauma & The Body

- **The Body Keeps the Score** — Bessel van der Kolk
  *The essential text on how trauma lives in the body. Aligns directly with Chapter 2's energetic signature work and Chapter 15's remapping.*

- **Waking the Tiger** — Peter Levine
  *Somatic experiencing — how the body processes and releases trauma. The theoretical foundation for much of this workbook's body-based approach.*

- **Complex PTSD: From Surviving to Thriving** — Pete Walker
  *If you grew up in a narcissistic family, your experience may better fit Complex PTSD than single-event trauma. This book names the four Fs: fight, flight, freeze, fawn.*

- **It Didn't Start with You** — Mark Wolynn
  *Inherited family trauma. If the patterns go back generations, this book helps you see the larger system.*

### On Attachment & Relationships

- **Attached** — Amir Levine & Rachel Heller
  *Attachment theory made accessible. Helps you understand why you chose who you chose — and how to choose differently.*

- **Hold Me Tight** — Sue Johnson
  *Emotionally Focused Therapy for couples. Useful for understanding what healthy attachment actually looks like.*

- **Facing Love Addiction** — Pia Mellody
  *If the trauma bond section hit home, this book goes deeper into the love addiction / love avoidance cycle.*

### On Family Systems

- **Adult Children of Emotionally Immature Parents** — Lindsay Gibson
  *If Chapters 7-10 resonated, this is your next read. Calm, clear, validating.*

- **Mothers Who Can't Love** — Susan Forward
  *Direct treatment of the narcissistic mother. Goes deeper into the archetypes introduced in Chapter 8.*

- **Toxic Parents** — Susan Forward
  *Broader than mothers — covers narcissistic fathers, controlling parents, and the process of confrontation and healing.*

- **The Drama of the Gifted Child** — Alice Miller
  *How gifted, sensitive children adapt to narcissistic parents — and how that adaptation shapes adulthood.*

### On Boundaries

- **Set Boundaries, Find Peace** — Nedra Glennon Tawwab
  *Practical boundary work. Complements Chapter 16's scripts and responses.*

- **Boundaries** — Henry Cloud & John Townsend
  *The foundational text on boundary-setting. Written from a Christian perspective but universally applicable.*

### On Shame & Self-Worth

- **Daring Greatly** — Brene Brown
  *Shame resilience and vulnerability. Aligns with the shame work in Chapter 15 and Section 14 of this workbook.*

- **Healing the Shame That Binds You** — John Bradshaw
  *Deep dive into toxic shame vs. healthy shame. If the shame inventory in Section 14 stirred things up, this goes further.*

## Support Communities

### Online Communities
- **r/NarcissisticAbuse** (Reddit) — Peer support community; moderated
- **r/raisedbynarcissists** (Reddit) — For those with narcissistic parents; heavily moderated and supportive
- **Out of the FOG** (outofthefog.website) — For people dealing with personality-disordered individuals; forums and resources
- **DailyStrength Narcissistic Personality Disorder Support Group** — Peer-moderated support

### Professional Support
- **Psychology Today Therapist Finder** (psychologytoday.com/us/therapists) — Search by specialization, including narcissistic abuse, trauma, EMDR
- **Open Path Collective** (openpathcollective.org) — Affordable therapy ($30-$80/session)
- **BetterHelp / Talkspace** — Online therapy platforms (insurance options vary)
- **SAMHSA Treatment Locator** (findtreatment.gov) — Free referral service

### When Looking for a Therapist
Ask these questions:
1. "Do you have experience working with survivors of narcissistic abuse?"
2. "Are you trained in trauma-informed approaches (EMDR, somatic experiencing, IFS)?"
3. "What is your approach to working with clients who are still in contact with their abuser?"
4. "Do you believe in the concept of narcissistic abuse?" *(Some clinicians don't. Find one who does.)*
