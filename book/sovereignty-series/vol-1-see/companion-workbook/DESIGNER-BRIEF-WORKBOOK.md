# Visual Design Brief: SEE — The Companion Workbook

## By Jennifer Brooke Lawless

### A Free Resource for Readers of *Recognizing Narcissistic Manipulation in Relationships, Family, and Work*

---

## Overview

This document provides the visual design specifications for the companion workbook to Volume 1 of The Sovereignty Series. The workbook is currently authored in markdown and needs professional layout design to meet the standards of published therapeutic workbooks (comparable to New Harbinger, Rockridge Press, and Callisto publications).

The workbook is **free** — distributed as a PDF download and potential print-on-demand resource. Despite being free, it must look and feel like a $20+ published workbook. The visual quality signals the author's professional credibility and drives sales of the main book.

---

## Design Philosophy

**Warm but clinical. Beautiful but functional. Inviting, never intimidating.**

The reader opening this workbook is likely in pain, possibly in crisis, and has survived (or is surviving) narcissistic abuse. The design must:

1. **Feel safe** — no jarring colors, no aggressive typography, no visual overwhelm
2. **Feel professional** — this is a therapeutic tool, not a coloring book
3. **Feel spacious** — generous margins, ample writing space, room to breathe
4. **Feel organized** — clear visual hierarchy so the reader always knows where they are
5. **Signal authority** — the author's M.S. credential and clinical background should be visually prominent

## Typography

### Recommended Font Pairings

**Option A (Warm & Clinical):**
- Headings: **Playfair Display** (serif, elegant, grounded)
- Body: **Source Sans Pro** (clean, readable, modern)
- Cards/Prompts: **IBM Plex Mono** (structured, clinical feel for cards)

**Option B (Modern & Approachable):**
- Headings: **Cormorant Garamond** (refined serif)
- Body: **Lato** (friendly sans-serif, excellent readability)
- Cards/Prompts: **Space Mono** (for reference cards and protocols)

### Type Hierarchy

| Element | Size (pt) | Weight | Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| Part Title (e.g., "Part I: Recognition") | 28-32 | Bold | Serif, uppercase or small caps |
| Section Title (e.g., "Section 1: The Shrinking Map") | 22-24 | Bold | Serif |
| Exercise Title (e.g., "Exercise 1A: Where Do You Feel Smaller?") | 18-20 | Semibold | Serif |
| Body Text | 11-12 | Regular | Sans-serif |

| Card Text | 10-11 | Regular | Monospace |
| Writing Prompts | 11 | Italic | Sans-serif |

## Page Layout

### Page Size

- **Print:** 8.5" x 11" (US Letter) — standard workbook size
- **PDF:** Same dimensions
- **Margins:** 1" top, 1.25" bottom, 1" sides (generous for writing comfort and spiral binding)

### Grid System

- **Single column** for body text and exercises
- **Two-column** for side-by-side exercises (e.g., "Who They Present vs. Who They Are")
- **Full-width tables** for tracking logs and inventories

### Writing Spaces

All blank lines for reader writing should be:
- **Ruled lines** (light gray, 0.3pt) — not blank white space
- **Line spacing:** 8mm between rules (generous for handwriting)
- **Minimum 4 lines** per writing prompt, 8-10 for freewrite exercises
- **Light gray dotted "write here" indicator** on the first line of each writing space

## Visual Elements

### 1. Part-Opening Pages (5 total)

Each Part gets a **full-page opening spread**:

- **Left page:** Part number and title in large serif type, centered
- **Right page:** The opening quote/blockquote that currently begins each Part
- **Background:** Subtle watermark pattern — the workbook's visual motif (see Motif section below)
- **Part accent color** as a colored bar along the top or spine edge

**Design reference:** Similar to Brene Brown's workbooks or the chapter openings in *The Complex PTSD Workbook* by Arielle Schwartz.

### 2. Colored Sidebars

Three types of sidebars, each with a distinct visual treatment:

**A. Safety Check Sidebar** (appears at the start of each Part)
- **Background:** Soft gradient from white to pale sage/amber/rose depending on safety level
- **Border:** Left-edge colored bar (GREEN/YELLOW/RED)
- **Icon:** Small shield or hand icon
- **Content:** The GREEN/YELLOW/RED safety triage text

**B. Grounding Pause Sidebar** (appears between heavy exercises)
- **Background:** Cream with teal left-edge accent bar
- **Icon:** Small lotus, wave, or breath icon
- **Content:** The grounding instructions currently in blockquotes (`> Grounding pause...`)
- **Typography:** Body text in italic, slightly smaller than main body

**C. Author Note Sidebar** (appears at end of each Part)
- **Background:** Warm cream with gold left-edge accent bar
- **Icon:** Small pen nib or heart icon
- **Content:** The encouragement text currently in blockquotes at Part endings
- **Typography:** Serif italic

### 3. Pull Quotes

Pull quotes are scattered throughout the workbook as emphasized statements. These should be:

- **Full-width** horizontal rule above and below
- **Centered text** in serif italic, 14-16pt
- **Accent color** for the horizontal rules (Part-specific accent)
- **Extra vertical padding** (20pt above and below)

**Examples of pull quotes from the text:**
- *"Genuine care makes you feel more yourself. Control disguised as care makes you feel less yourself."*
- *"Naming breaks the spell."*
- *"My sensitivity was never the problem. My sensitivity was accurate."*
- *"You were not too sensitive. You were not reading into things. You were not overreacting."*
- *"Grief doesn't follow a timeline. It comes in waves."*
- *"Your body is not overreacting. Your body is reporting."*

### 4. Designed Cards (12 cards in Appendix F + inline cards in Section 13)

The current ASCII-art cards must be replaced with **professionally designed reference cards**. Specifications:

**Card Dimensions:**
- **Standard card:** 3.5" x 5" (index card size) — fits in a wallet, phone case, or notebook
- **Full-page card:** 8" x 5" for cards with more content (Body Decoder, Response Bank)

**Card Design Elements:**
- **Rounded corners** (8px radius in digital, die-cut in print)
- **Colored header bar** with card title in white text
- **Body text** in clean sans-serif, left-aligned
- **Key phrases** in bold or accent color
- **Border:** 2pt colored border matching Part accent color
- **Dashed cut line** around each card with scissors icon
- **Card number** in small type in lower-right corner

### 5. Tables and Tracking Logs

The workbook contains numerous tables for tracking (body awareness journals, cycle trackers, 72-Hour Test logs, energy audits). These need:

- **Alternating row shading** (white / very light cream)
- **Colored header row** matching Part accent color
- **Cell padding:** Generous (at least 12pt vertical) to allow handwriting
- **For multi-week logs:** Each week gets a subtle section divider
- **Checkbox columns:** Use actual checkbox squares, not text `[ ]`

### 6. Checklists

Multiple exercises use checklist format (family roles, love-bombing indicators, etc.). Design as:

- **Custom checkbox** — rounded square, 16pt, with Part accent color border
- **Slightly indented text** after checkbox
- **Grouped** visually with category headings in bold

### 7. Rating Scales

Several exercises use rating scales (0-3, 0-5, 1-10, -3 to +3). Design as:

- **Horizontal scale bar** with numbered markers
- **Color gradient** from light to dark (or cool to warm) across the scale
- **Circle markers** at each integer point
- **Labels** at endpoints

### 8. Emotion Spectrum Lines (Exercise 16B)

The contradictory emotions exercise uses horizontal lines between opposing emotions. Design as:

- **Gradient bar** from one emotion color to another
- **Slider marker** that the reader can mark with a pen
- **Labels** at each end in serif italic
- **Writing space** below each spectrum

## Recurring Visual Motif

**Suggested motif: The Light/Sovereignty Symbol**

Options:
- A geometric light pattern (rays emanating from a point) — connects to "See" and "seeing clearly"
- A minimalist eye motif — subtle, not literal
- An expanding circle pattern — represents moving from contraction to expansion
- A compass or cardinal directions — represents finding your own North

The motif should appear:
- Watermarked on Part-opening pages
- As a small icon next to Part numbers
- On the cover and title page
- Subtly in the footer of each page

## Page-by-Page Design Notes

### Welcome / Front Matter (00-welcome.md)
- **Title page:** Full bleed, workbook title large, author name with M.S., series identification
- **Safety triage table:** Colored rows (green/yellow/red) with clear visual differentiation
- **Crisis resources:** Boxed section with red accent border — this must be visually prominent
- **Progress tracker:** Designed as a visual checklist with checkboxes and Part accent colors

### Part Opening Pages
- Each Part starts on a **recto (right-hand) page**
- Full-page design with Part number, title, and opening quote
- Part accent color as header bar

### Exercise Pages
- **Exercise title** in accent-colored header bar
- **Instructions** in a lightly shaded box
- **Writing spaces** with ruled lines
- **Tables** with generous cell height for handwriting

### Appendix F: Printable Cards
- **Perforated pages** (in print version) or clearly marked cut lines
- Cards arranged 2-per-page for standard printing
- Each card designed to be individually useful when separated from the book

## Production Notes

- **Page count estimate:** 180-220 pages (based on current content length with designed layout)
- **Paper stock recommendation:** 70-80lb uncoated cream text (warm, easy to write on)
- **Binding:** Spiral or lay-flat binding strongly preferred (readers need both hands free to write)
- **Color printing:** Full-color interior (essential for safety level indicators and card designs)
- **Alternative:** If budget requires B&W interior, use grayscale versions of accent colors and ensure safety levels are distinguishable through pattern/icon, not just color
