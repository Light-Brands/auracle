# Appendix A: Quick Reference — All 60 Decoder Patterns

> One-page summary of each pattern with the power move.
> Keep this reference nearby. When something confusing happens, scan the list.
> Naming breaks the spell.

---

## How to Use This Reference

When you feel confused, activated, or "off" after an interaction:

1. Scan this list
2. Find the pattern that matches
3. Read the power move
4. Breathe

You don't need to identify the exact card. A rough match is enough. The act of naming is what breaks the loop.

> **Start with your top 10.** There are 60 patterns here. That is intentionally comprehensive — and intentionally more than you need right now. Do not try to learn them all at once. Instead: scan the full list once, slowly. Circle or highlight the 10 patterns you recognize most from your own experience. Those are *your* decoder cards. Learn their names. Practice their power moves. The other 50 will be here when you are ready. Most people find that 10-15 patterns account for the vast majority of what they experience. Find yours first.

## Card 0: The Master Card — The Wolf in Sheep's Clothing

**The Pattern:** Every manipulation tactic shares one thing — control disguised as something virtuous (care, honesty, protection, love).

**The Test:** After this interaction, do I feel more myself or less myself?

**Power Move:** Stop looking at the clothing. Start noticing the impact.

## Part 1: Core Patterns (Cards 1–27)

| Card | Pattern | What It Looks Like | Power Move |
|------|---------|-------------------|------------|
| 1 | Interruption as Dominance | Disrupting your focus, creating "emergencies" during your alone time | "I'm going to finish this. We can talk when I'm done." |
| 2 | "We Need to Talk" Urgency | Ominous announcements, manufactured crises | "About what specifically?" Refuse the anxiety hook. |
| 3 | Volume as Victory | Using loudness to overwhelm, then denying aggression | "I'll continue this conversation at a normal volume." Leave if they don't. |
| 4 | Physical Intimidation | Standing too close, looming, blocking exits, using size to control | Create distance. Trust the discomfort signal. |
| 5 | Chaos as Camouflage | Creating confusion and disorder so you can't think clearly | "I don't need to understand your logic to trust my experience." |
| 6 | Shame Inversion | Making you feel defective for having normal needs or reactions | "My needs are not the problem." |
| 7 | Mockery Voice | Imitating you in a mocking tone to diminish your words | "I said what I said. Repeating it in a funny voice doesn't change it." |
| 8 | Financial Superiority | Using money as leverage, keeping score, controlling access to resources | "My worth is not determined by my bank account — or yours." |
| 9 | Trivial Demand Tests | Small, unreasonable requests to test compliance before bigger ones | "No." (It's a complete sentence.) |
| 10 | The False Urgency | Manufacturing time pressure so you decide before you can think | "I'll decide on my own timeline." |
| 11 | The Aesthetic Seesaw | Alternating compliments and criticism about your appearance to keep you off-balance | "I'm comfortable with how I look." |
| 12 | Selective Idealization | Praising you to others while devaluing you in private (or vice versa) | Notice the gap. Trust the private version — that's the real one. |
| 13 | Moving Goalposts | Criteria for approval constantly changing so you can never succeed | "I've met the original request. The conversation is over." |
| 14 | Comparison Triangulation | Bringing in a third person to create insecurity or competition | "I'd prefer to discuss this between us." |
| 15 | Conditional Affection | Love that arrives only when you comply, perform, or submit | Notice: Is this warmth, or is this a reward for obedience? |
| 16 | The Gaslight Loop | "That never happened" / "You're imagining things" / rewriting reality | "I know what I experienced." Don't debate reality. |
| 17 | Projection | Accusing you of exactly what they're doing | Name it silently. Don't defend against their mirror. |
| 18 | Minimization | "It wasn't that bad" / "You're overreacting" | "My experience of it is valid." |
| 19 | Righteous Justification | Framing harmful behavior as morally correct — "I did it for your own good" | "I'll decide what's good for me." |
| 20 | Reversal of Blame | Turning the conversation so you're defending yourself instead of addressing their behavior | "We were talking about your behavior, not mine." |
| 21 | Weaponized Humor | "It was just a joke" — cruelty disguised as comedy | "If it's funny, why does my body tighten when you say it?" |
| 22 | Sexualized Dominance | Using sexual dynamics to control, withhold, or pressure | Your body is yours. Desire that comes with conditions isn't desire — it's leverage. |
| 23 | Withholding Tenderness | Withdrawing affection as punishment without saying why | Don't chase. The withdrawal is the message. |
| 24 | Love-Bombing | Overwhelming early affection, intensity that feels like destiny | Apply the 72-Hour Test. Slow down. Real love doesn't require urgency. |
| 25 | Body Shaming Disguised as Preference | "I just like you better when..." — criticism framed as taste | "My body is not your project." |
| 26 | Energy Invalidation | "You're too sensitive" / "You're too much" / dismissing your experience | "My sensitivity is accurate. I'm detecting something real." |
| 27 | Control Through Fatigue | Exhausting you until you stop fighting back | Recognize the strategy. Rest is resistance. |

| 31 | Authority Hijack | Invoking an expert, a therapist, a pastor, or "everyone thinks so" to override your perception | "I trust my own experience." |

| 34 | "You'd Be Lucky to Have Me" | Inflating their value while diminishing yours | "I know my worth. It doesn't depend on your assessment." |
| 35 | Withholding as Punishment | Silence, cold shoulder, emotional withdrawal — all without explanation | Name it: "This is punishment, not processing." |
| 36 | Withholding Belongings as Control | Keeping your things, your money, your documents to maintain leverage | Document what's yours. Secure what you can. |
| 37 | Concern as Control | "I'm only saying this because I love you" — surveillance disguised as care | "I appreciate the concern. I've got this." |
| 38 | The "Huh?" Gaslight Loop | Pretending not to understand something perfectly clear to exhaust you | "I've explained this clearly. I'm not going to explain it again." |

| 40 | Information Control | Controlling what you know, who you talk to, what you're allowed to access | Your information is yours. Secure your accounts. |
| 41 | Strategic Vulnerability | Showing "weakness" to disarm you or avoid accountability | Notice: Does this vulnerability create change or just buy time? |
| 42 | The False Apology | "I'm sorry you feel that way" — an apology that apologizes for nothing | "That's not an apology. An apology names what you did." |
| 43 | Future Faking | Promises of change that never materialize — "Soon we'll..." / "Next time I'll..." | Watch behavior, not words. Track the pattern. |
| 44 | The Victim Narrative | Every situation, they're the one who's hurt — permanent victim status | Ask: "What's your part in this?" |
| 45 | Boundary Testing | Small violations to see what you'll tolerate before escalating | Hold the line the first time. Every time. |
| 46 | Hoovering | Returning after discard with charm, tears, or crisis to pull you back in | Remember why you left. Don't respond from activation. |
| 47 | Playing the Long Game | Patient, incremental erosion — so slow you don't notice until you're diminished | Zoom out. Compare who you are now to who you were before them. |
| 48 | Flying Monkeys | Recruiting others to pressure, guilt, or surveil you on their behalf | Set boundaries with the messengers too. |
| 49 | The Grand Gesture | Dramatic displays of love or remorse that substitute for actual change | Gestures are not growth. Ask: "What's different structurally?" |
| 50 | Identity Erosion | Slowly reshaping who you are to serve their needs | Write down who you were before. Return to them. |
| 51 | The Final Discard | Abandonment designed to maximize your devastation and their narrative | You were not discarded. You were released. |
| 52 | Narrative Reclamation | Rewriting the story of the relationship so they're the hero or the victim | Your version is valid. Write it down. Keep it. |
| 53 | Legal Threat as Leverage | Using lawyers, courts, or legal language to intimidate and control | Document everything. Consult your own attorney. Don't be intimidated into silence. |

## The One Question That Covers Everything

When you can't identify the specific pattern, return to Card 0:

> *After this interaction, do I feel more myself or less myself?*

Genuine care makes you larger. Control makes you smaller.

That's the only decoder you need.
