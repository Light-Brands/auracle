# SEE: The Companion Workbook

### A Free Resource for Readers of *The Truth That Was Hidden in Plain Sight*

---

> This workbook is designed to be used alongside Volume 1 of The Sovereignty Series.
> It expands on the light prompts woven through the book with guided exercises,
> pattern-mapping tools, and space to do the deeper work at your own pace.

---

## How to Use This Workbook

This workbook follows the structure of the book. Each section corresponds to specific chapters, and the exercises build on what you've just read. But there's no required order, no test at the end, and no wrong way to use it.

**Some suggestions:**

- **Read the chapter first, then do the exercises.** The book opens the door. This workbook helps you walk through it.
- **Go in order, or don't.** If a particular section calls to you, start there. Your nervous system often knows what it needs before your mind does.
- **Write by hand if you can.** Research suggests that handwriting engages the brain differently than typing — it slows you down and connects thought to body. But if typing is what's accessible, use that.
- **Don't force it.** If an exercise feels too activating, skip it. Come back later. Or don't. There is no obligation here.
- **Use a separate journal.** Some exercises need more space than the page allows. Keep a notebook dedicated to this work.

**What this workbook is:**
- A space for your own truth
- A set of tools for seeing more clearly
- A companion for the work you're already doing

**What this workbook is not:**
- Therapy (though it can support therapy)
- A diagnostic tool
- A weapon to use against anyone
- Something that needs to be completed

## A Note on Pacing

There is no deadline for seeing clearly.

Some of these exercises will feel straightforward. Others will take you to places you haven't visited in a long time — or ever. Both responses are normal.

**Signs you may need to slow down:**
- You feel emotionally flooded or numb
- Your body is tense, shaking, or exhausted
- You can't stop crying, or you can't cry at all
- You feel dissociated — like you're watching yourself from a distance
- You have an urge to contact the person you're writing about

**When this happens:**
- Close the workbook
- Use the grounding techniques on page [Appendix C]
- Do something that connects you to the present: walk, cook, hold something cold, call someone safe
- Come back when you're ready. The workbook will wait.

You don't have to do this all at once. You don't have to do it at all. But if you do, do it gently.

## Safety First: When to Pause, When to Reach Out

This workbook asks you to look at painful patterns. That looking can stir things up. Before you begin, please read this section carefully.

### Assess Your Current Safety

| Safety Level | Description | Workbook Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **GREEN** | You are physically safe, out of the relationship, or have strong support | Work through exercises at your own pace |
| **YELLOW** | You are still in the situation but not in immediate danger; some support exists | Work through exercises selectively; avoid sections that could trigger confrontation |
| **RED** | You are in danger, being monitored, or have no support system | **Prioritize your safety above all else.** Do not leave this workbook where it can be found. Use crisis resources below. |

### If You Are in RED

**Stop here and get support first.**

This workbook will be here when you're safe. Your life comes first.

- **National Domestic Violence Hotline:** 1-800-799-7233 (24/7)
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text **HOME** to **741741**
- **National Suicide Prevention Lifeline:** **988**
- **Emergency Services:** **911**

If you cannot safely store this workbook, use the online version at [website URL] in a private/incognito browser window. Clear your browser history afterward.

### If You Are in YELLOW

Work selectively. Focus on:
- Body awareness exercises (Section 2)
- Grounding techniques (Appendix C)
- Emergency protocol cards (Section 13)
- Scripts and responses (Section 15)

Avoid exercises that involve writing about the person if they could find what you've written.

### If You Are in GREEN

You have the space to go deeper. Use the full workbook. Go slowly anyway.
