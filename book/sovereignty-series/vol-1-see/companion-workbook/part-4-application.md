# Part IV: Application

### Chapters 11--14 — Applying What You See

---

> You've learned the language. You've mapped the patterns.
> Now comes the part that changes things: using what you know in the relationships you're actually living.

---

This is where the workbook shifts from understanding to doing. The exercises in this section ask you to look at specific relationships, run real experiments, and build tools you can carry with you — literally, in your wallet or your phone — for the moments when your clarity goes offline.

A reminder before you begin: these exercises may bring up strong feelings. That's not a sign you're doing it wrong. That's a sign you're doing it. Go at your own pace. Use the grounding tools you've been building. And if you need to stop, stop. This section will be here when you come back.

## Section 10: The Relationship Timeline

**Aligned with Chapter 11 — Romantic Manipulation**

Chapter 11 names the three phases of narcissistic romance: **Idealization** (love-bombing), **Devaluation** (criticism, withdrawal, intermittent reinforcement), and **Discard/Cycling** (abandonment that pulls you back in). Most people can see these phases clearly in retrospect. The hard part is that while you're inside them, each phase has a different name — one that sounds like love.

This exercise helps you map those phases onto an actual relationship. If you've been in more than one relationship like this, start with the one that's easiest to see clearly. You can always come back and map the others.

---

## Section 12: Your Exposure Questions

**Aligned with Chapter 13 — The Exposure Questions**

Chapter 13 introduces a set of questions that function like a diagnostic — not of the other person, but of the dynamic. Exposure questions don't accuse. They don't attack. They simply create conditions where empathy, accountability, and self-awareness either show up or don't.

But these questions are not toys. Asking them in the wrong context or at the wrong time can escalate a dangerous situation. Before you proceed, complete the safety assessment below.

---
