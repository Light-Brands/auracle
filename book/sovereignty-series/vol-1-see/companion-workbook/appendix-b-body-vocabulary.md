# Appendix B: Body Sensation Vocabulary

> Your body speaks a language you were taught to ignore. This vocabulary helps you translate.
>
> Use this list when you feel "something" but can't name it. Scan the categories.
> Notice which words make your body respond. That's your body recognizing its own language.

---

## How to Use This List

1. After an interaction that left you feeling "off," scan these categories
2. Circle or highlight the sensations that match your experience
3. Over time, you'll build your own body vocabulary — the specific signals your body uses to communicate
4. Use this vocabulary in the exercises throughout the workbook

---

## Tension & Tightness

| Sensation | Where You Might Feel It | What It May Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Clenching | Jaw, fists, stomach | Anger being held back, bracing for impact |
| Tightness | Chest, throat, shoulders | Feeling pressured, controlled, or unsafe |
| Constriction | Throat, chest | Words being swallowed, truth being suppressed |
| Bracing | Whole body, especially core | Anticipating an attack or mood shift |
| Rigidity | Neck, back, limbs | Hypervigilance, scanning for danger |
| Armoring | Chest, upper body | Protecting the heart space |
| Gripping | Stomach, hands | Holding on, refusing to let go of control |
| Locking | Knees, jaw, shoulders | Freezing in place, unable to move |

| Emptiness | Chest, stomach | Grief, abandonment, emotional depletion |
| Heaviness | Whole body, limbs, chest | Depression, carrying what isn't yours, exhaustion |
| Deadness | Arms, legs, face | Dissociation, freeze response |
| Hollowness | Chest, stomach | The absence of something that should be there |
| Flatness | Whole body, emotional landscape | Shutdown after sustained overwhelm |

## Urgency & Activation

| Sensation | Where You Might Feel It | What It May Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Restlessness | Legs, whole body | Fight-or-flight wanting to discharge |
| Racing heart | Chest | Activation, fear, trauma response |
| Shallow breathing | Upper chest | Anxiety, hypervigilance |
| Held breath | Chest, throat | Waiting for the other shoe to drop |
| Nausea | Stomach | Revulsion, boundary being crossed, deep knowing |
| Urgency to explain | Throat, chest | Being misunderstood on purpose, gaslighting |
| Urgency to flee | Legs, whole body | Your body saying "this isn't safe" |
| Urgency to fix | Hands, chest | Fawn response, caretaking programming |

## Relief & Safety

| Sensation | Where You Might Feel It | What It May Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Softening | Shoulders, jaw, belly | Relaxation, safety, trust |
| Opening | Chest, throat | Being seen, being safe to speak |
| Deepening breath | Whole torso | Nervous system settling |
| Tears | Eyes, throat, chest | Release — of grief, relief, or recognition |
| Lightness | Chest, shoulders | Burden lifting, truth landing |
| Groundedness | Feet, legs, core | Present, stable, connected to self |
| Warmth (safe) | Chest, hands | Genuine connection, love, belonging |
| Stillness | Whole body | Peace — not numbness, but calm |

---

## The Key Distinction

**Numbness is not calm.**

- Numbness is the absence of feeling — a protective shutdown.
- Calm is the presence of safety — your nervous system at rest.

If you can't tell the difference yet, that's okay. Your body will teach you as you practice.

## Building Your Personal Vocabulary

Over the next week, use these prompts to start mapping your body's unique language:

**My body's "danger" signal is usually:** _______________________________________________

**I feel it most often in my:** _______________________________________________

**My body's "safe" signal feels like:** _______________________________________________

**The sensation I've been ignoring most is:** _______________________________________________

**When I'm around [person], my body usually does:** _______________________________________________

**When I'm around [safe person], my body usually does:** _______________________________________________

**The word I've been using instead of naming what I feel:** _______________________________________________

*(Most people default to "fine," "stressed," "tired," or "anxious." Your body has more specific things to say.)*
